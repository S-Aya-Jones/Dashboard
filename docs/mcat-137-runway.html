How an hour is legally spent

There are only three shapes a block can take. Nothing else counts as study.

Content: learn topic → make Anki cards on it immediately → do 5–10 discretes on that exact topic → every miss becomes a card. Never just read.

Passage: do it timed, no pausing. Then review longer than you did it — say why the right answer is right and why each wrong one is a trap.

Review: every miss gets a cause-tag. The tag is the fix.

gap → make a card misread → slow the stem careless → checklist timing → pacing drill trap → study the pattern

The one metric that tells the truth: your AAMC full-length average in the final month. To be a 520, those FLs need to land 518+ before test day. If a month out you're at 510, that's the honest signal to push the date or recalibrate — no plan guarantees the number, it just maximizes the odds and tells you early.

The trap for your exact profile: over-investing in content, under-investing in passages + review. If you ever catch yourself rereading a chapter instead of doing tagged passages, the plan has quietly broken. Encode → apply under timing → diagnose every miss → patch the exact hole. That loop is the whole machine.
